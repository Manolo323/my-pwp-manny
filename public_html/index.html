The first step to learning guitar is by learning the strings, their tuning, and the frets. Standard tuning for most six-string guitars is:
E (6th string), A (5th string), D (4th string), G (3rd string), B (2nd string), and high E (1st string) from thinnest to thickest, as shown in the image. The frets are the metal bars that divide the fretboard into different sections. The fretboard is the long, narrow part of the guitar where the strings and the bridge connect to the guitar.

Once you have a basic understanding of the strings, tuning, and frets, it's important to know how to hold the guitar correctly. For right-handed players, position your guitar on your right leg. Avoid slouching the guitar towards you to see your fingers, as this can cause fatigue and poor posture. Instead, keep the guitar upright and straight. Another tip is to bring your strumming arm tightly around the guitar to maintain a good position for extended practice and play.

Pro Tip: A great way to remember the notes of each string is to use the mnemonic method. Just remember:

Make sure your fingers are pressing down firmly on the strings and that they're not touching any other strings. This will ensure that each string rings out clearly when you strum the chord. Strum all six strings with your right hand or pick. Be sure to only strum the strings you're holding down with your left hand. If any of the strings are muted or not ringing out clearly, adjust your fingers until each string sounds clean. With practice, you'll be able fingerpick string and switch smoothly between different chords.

Keep practicing and have fun!
