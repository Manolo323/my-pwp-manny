My name is Manny and I've been playing the guitar for 13 years. As a beginner, I understand how intimidating it can be to pick up a new instrument. I struggled to find easily available content that provided clear and detailed instructions for playing guitar. However, now that I have been playing for a while, I know that with the right guidance and plenty of practice, anyone can learn to play the guitar. In this beginner lesson, my goal is to create a relaxed and supportive environment where anyone can feel comfortable making mistakes while still learning and improving their skills.

The first step to learning how to play the guitar is by learning the strings, their tuning, and the frets. Standard tuning, which is used for most six-string guitars, consists of the strings tuned to the pitches E, A, D, G, B, and E. Each string is numbered from the thickest to the the thinnest, beginning with the low E (6th string) and ending with the high E (1st string), as shown in the accompanying image. When you press down on a string at a specific fret, you can change the pitch of the string. The frets are the metal bars that divide the fretboard into different sections. The fretboard is the long, narrow part of the guitar where the strings and the bridge connect to the guitar.

Once you have a basic understanding of the strings, tuning, and frets, it's important to know how to hold the guitar correctly. Begin by sitting in a chair with a straight back and placing the guitar on your lap. Rest your dominant hand's forearm on top of the guitar while your non-dominant hand is on the fretboard. Your dominant hand should be on the strings near the bridge, allowing you to strum and pluck the strings with ease.

Make sure your fingers are pressing down firmly on the strings and that they're not touching any other strings. This will ensure that each string rings out clearly when you strum the chord. Strum all six strings with your right hand or pick. Be sure to only strum the strings you're holding down with your left hand. If any of the strings are muted or not ringing out clearly, adjust your fingers until each string sounds clean. With practice, you'll be able to switch smoothly between different chords.
Keep practicing and have fun!
